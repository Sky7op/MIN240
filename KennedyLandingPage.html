Design process

Making this a reality
The idea of this project began when I rented a camera to use on a short film shoot. Having even myself navagating endless options with little to no suggestive power, I began to think of the average user who may need better equipment, but may not have all of the technical specs of all equipment memorized.

This lead to me speaking to a photographer that I frequently work with what she would look for on a renting site when looking for equipment.

The things that stood out to me were:

1. Easy navigation for each kind of product.
2. Suggestions based on what you need.
3. Professional chat support.
Insight Statement
Proper implementation of browsing utilities could simplify the camera renting process, from professionals to amateur users.

Although the individuals in my group did not have personal experience in renting camera gear, they did seem to agree in my explaining that the current norm for rentals is confusing and difficult if you don't already know what you're looking for specifically and could use some simplification.

Questions to answer:
How can I make it easy to find what a user needs based on their project?
What questions do I need to ask in order to determine a good recommendation?
Who is this targeted to?
How does this overall help the user?
